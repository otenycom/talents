# Food Ideas — meals & staples that hit the protocol

A pantry of meals and staple foods that satisfy the levers in `protocol.md`: high
protein with the per-meal leucine threshold met, high fiber, dark berries, extra-virgin
olive oil (EVOO) for fat, omega-3 fish, and **no ultra-processed food, no alcohol, and
refined/starchy carbs kept minimal**.

These are **examples, not a fixed menu.** The bot is free to look up or generate
equivalent items — regional dishes, seasonal produce, vegetarian swaps — as long as the
swap respects the same constraints:

- Each protein-anchored meal delivers **25–40 g protein / 2.5–3.0 g leucine** (the mTOR
  threshold). Plant proteins are lower in leucine, so a vegetarian swap needs a larger
  portion **or** a whey / soy-isolate top-up to clear the threshold.
- Day totals land in the tenant's band: **1.6–2.2 g/kg/day protein across 3–5 meals**,
  **30–40 g fiber**, **200–300 g dark berries**, **2–3 tbsp EVOO**, **2–3 g omega-3**.
- Respect anything in the tenant's `profile` — allergies, dislikes, dietary pattern
  (halal/kosher/vegetarian). Scale every portion to *their* protein target; never copy
  an example gram amount blindly.

## Staple anchors (the workhorses)

These three do most of the heavy lifting — keep them stocked.

- **Protein-yoghurt bowl** (the "fruit bowl"). ~300 g lean Greek yoghurt or skyr
  (**9–11% protein, low-fat**) + **10–20 g whey** + **200–300 g dark berries**
  (blue / black / raspberry) + chia seeds + **psyllium husk** + a little açai powder.
  One bowl clears a meal's protein/leucine **and** contributes a large share of the
  daily fiber and polyphenols. Yoghurt is casein-rich (~9–10% leucine); the whey tops
  it past threshold cheaply.
- **Cottage cheese (Hüttenkäse).** 100–250 g as a fast protein/leucine anchor —
  casein, slow-release, ~9–10% leucine. Eats well with berries, with smoked fish, or
  with a little whey stirred in. A reliable snack or breakfast component.
- **Whey protein.** The leucine king (~10–11% leucine of protein). **10–20 g** stirred
  into yoghurt, skyr, or cottage cheese lifts almost any meal to the leucine threshold
  for very few calories — the cheapest way to fix a protein-light meal.
- **Psyllium husk.** 1 tsp–1 tbsp/day toward the 30–40 g fiber target. Beyond the VAT
  benefit it supports **bowel regularity and the gut/stress axis** (a calmer gut helps
  the cortisol picture the protocol cares about). Always take it with plenty of water.

## Protein sources (leucine-dense)

- **Eggs** — fried in EVOO or boiled, 3–4 per protein meal; ~8.6% leucine.
- **Poultry** — chicken breast & thigh fillet (grilled/baked in EVOO), smoked chicken
  breast, chicken mince / chicken balls, turkey breast; ~8% leucine. 200–350 g cooked
  clears a meal easily.
- **Fish (lean + omega-3)** — tuna (in water or EVOO), salmon (poached or smoked),
  mackerel, sardines, anchovies, herring, smoked eel, white fish (cod, seabass,
  dorade), tuna steak.
- **Lean red meat** — lean beef / Angus, steak, minced beef; occasional, leaner cuts.
- **Dairy** — high-protein Greek yoghurt / skyr (9–11%), cottage cheese, whey; a small
  amount of hard cheese (goat / Gouda / Parmesan) as a garnish, not a portion.
- **Vegetarian swaps** — tofu, tempeh, edamame, high-protein soy yoghurt, legumes +
  whey/soy-isolate. These need larger portions or a leucine top-up to reach 2.5–3.0 g.

## Vegetables & fiber

- **Leafy** — spinach, kale, rucola (arugula).
- **Cruciferous** — cauliflower, broccoli, cabbage / coleslaw (raw), Brussels sprouts.
- **Other** — peas, green beans, asparagus, fennel, mushrooms, onion, tomato, peppers,
  celery, courgette.
- **Fiber boosters** — psyllium husk, chia seeds, dark berries (also count toward the
  berry/polyphenol anchor).

## Fats

- **EVOO** for cooking and dressings (2–3 tbsp/day).
- **Avocado.**
- **Nuts** — a small handful of walnuts / almonds / pecans (calorie-dense; a garnish,
  not a meal).

## Berries & polyphenols

- Blueberries, blackberries, raspberries — **200–300 g/day**.
- Açai powder as a polyphenol top-up in the bowl.

## Low-sugar dressings

- EVOO + vinegar or balsamic.
- Lean yoghurt + Dijon mustard + garlic + lemon juice.

## Meal templates (mix and match)

- **Breakfast** — 3–4 eggs (fried in EVOO or boiled) + a cold-water fish or smoked
  chicken breast + leafy greens with oil & vinegar. **Or** the protein-yoghurt bowl.
- **Lunch** — a big veg-forward plate: grilled/baked chicken (breast or thigh) in EVOO
  over rucola/greens with an EVOO-vinegar or yoghurt-mustard dressing; **or** a
  chicken-and-vegetable soup on a white-wine-reduction base (alcohol cooked off);
  **or** chicken saag without naan or rice.
- **Dinner** — a 200–350 g lean protein anchor (chicken thigh, turkey, white fish, lean
  beef) + a large pile of cruciferous/leafy veg + EVOO. Keep starch minimal.
- **Snack** — smoked chicken breast + cottage cheese; boiled eggs; cottage cheese +
  berries; yoghurt + whey (cocoa for a "brownie" flavour); a small handful of nuts.

## What to keep off the plate

- **Refined / starchy carbs by default** — rice, naan, bread, large potato portions.
  Keep occasional and small; lean on vegetables for volume.
- **Ultra-processed foods** and sugary sauces.
- **Alcohol** — cooking reductions only (alcohol cooked off), never as a drink.
- **One giant protein meal** — distribute protein across 3–5 meals so each clears the
  leucine threshold; a skewed intake wastes ~25% of the daily muscle-protein response.
